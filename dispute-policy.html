Dispute Policy

This policy describes how BlackCrow can handle disputes between buyers and sellers in social commerce transactions. It is a launch draft and should be reviewed before public use.

When a Dispute Can Happen

A dispute may happen when a buyer does not receive the item, the seller believes delivery was completed, the item is damaged, the item does not match the agreed description, or a release condition is contested.

Terms Come First

Before funding, both parties should accept the transaction terms: item category, condition, quantity, delivery method, courier or dispatch channel, shipping responsibility, release condition, and inspection window. These terms are the first reference point during review.

Dispatch Proof

For physical goods, the seller should submit the courier or dispatch channel, waybill or tracking number, dispatch note, and any receipt, packaging photo, handover photo, or short video link before marking delivery. If a seller uses a bus park, rider, or third-party dispatcher, the seller is still responsible for providing clear dispatch evidence.

What BlackCrow Reviews

BlackCrow may review escrow terms, buyer and seller acceptance, funding confirmation, dispatch proof, delivery notes, release condition, timeline events, payment provider records, and evidence submitted by both parties.

Buyer Responsibilities

The buyer should inspect the product or service within the agreed period, keep delivery or unboxing evidence where possible, and avoid releasing funds until the agreed condition is met.

Seller Responsibilities

The seller should provide accurate item details, dispatch evidence, delivery evidence, and payout details. The seller should not request withdrawal until the buyer has released funds.

Funds During Review

When a dispute is opened, release and withdrawal actions are paused while BlackCrow reviews the submitted evidence. The goal is to keep funds neutral until the facts are clearer.

Possible Outcomes

Depending on evidence and payment-provider capabilities, BlackCrow may support release, refund, partial settlement, extended review, or escalation to manual support.

Support Contact

Dispute messages should go to disputes@blackcrow.com with the escrow ID, buyer email, seller email, and a short explanation.
