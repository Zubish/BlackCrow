Dispute Policy

This policy describes how BlackCrow can handle disputes between buyers and sellers. It is a launch draft and should be reviewed before public use.

When a Dispute Can Happen

A dispute may happen when a buyer does not receive the item, the seller believes delivery was completed, the item does not match the agreed description, or a release condition is contested.

What BlackCrow Reviews

BlackCrow may review escrow terms, buyer and seller acceptance, funding confirmation, delivery notes, release condition, timeline events, payment provider records, and evidence submitted by both parties.

Buyer Responsibilities

The buyer should inspect the product or service within the agreed period and avoid releasing funds until the agreed condition is met.

Seller Responsibilities

The seller should provide accurate item details, delivery evidence, and payout details. The seller should not request withdrawal until the buyer has released funds.

Possible Outcomes

Depending on evidence and payment-provider capabilities, BlackCrow may support release, refund, partial settlement, extended review, or escalation to manual support.

Support Contact

Dispute messages should go to disputes@blackcrow.com with the escrow ID, buyer email, seller email, and a short explanation.
